    Prevention for inadequate
    clean water and sanitation

    Access to Safe Drinking Water:
    Ensure availability of clean and safe drinking water by treating and filtering water sources.
    Improved Sanitation Facilities:
    Build and maintain proper sanitation facilities to prevent human waste from contaminating water supplies.
    Hygiene Education:
    Promote handwashing with soap, safe food handling, and personal hygiene practices.
    Water Treatment:
    Use methods such as boiling, chlorination, or filtration to treat water before consumption.
    Proper Waste Disposal:
    Implement efficient waste management systems to prevent environmental contamination.
    Infrastructure Development:
    Invest in infrastructure to ensure reliable access to clean water and sanitation facilities.
    #Implementing these measures can significantly reduce the incidence of water-related diseases and improve overall public health.

    WRIS

    Comprehensive Approach

    Community participation

    Encourage community involvement in planning, decision-making, and management of water resources and sanitation services to ensure that solutions are culturally appropriate and widely accepted.

    International Cooperation

    Foster collaboration between countries, particularly in transboundary water management, to share resources, knowledge, and technologies for mutual benefit.

    Legislation and Policy

    Develop and enforce laws and regulations to protect water resources and ensure sustainable usage.

    Ensuring Accessibility

    Build and maintain water supply infrastructure, including pipelines, reservoirs, and distribution systems.

    Financial and Technical Support

    Secure funding from national governments, international organizations, and private sectors for water and sanitation projects.

    Integrated Water Resource Management (IWRM)

    Adopt an integrated approach to manage water resources, considering the interconnections between water supply, quality, and ecosystem health.
